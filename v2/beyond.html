Beyond the Data

Beyond academics and technical work, I am shaped by experiences that influence how I think, compete, and solve problems. These moments in nature, travel, and leadership continue to strengthen how I approach sustainability challenges with curiosity, structure, and impact.

Nature 🌲

I’m at my best when I’m outside—walking, hiking, or just taking in the quiet details of a place. Nature keeps me grounded, and it’s the reason sustainability isn’t just an “interest” for me… it’s personal.

That connection is also what drives my work: using data and technology to make environmental decisions clearer, more measurable, and more impactful in the real world.

Travel 🌍

I love exploring new places—especially the parts that don’t show up on a checklist. Travel reminds me that every community has its own rhythm, priorities, and relationship with the environment.

Visiting Saudi Arabia broadened my worldview and deepened my appreciation for cultural and environmental diversity. It shaped how I think about sustainability: context matters, and solutions need to respect people and place.

Problem Solving & Leadership 🧩

I genuinely enjoy messy problems—the kind that need structure, creativity, and follow-through. Whether it’s a class project or a student initiative, I like organizing ideas into a plan people can actually execute.

From leading an E-waste campaign to supporting TEDx sponsorship work, I’ve learned how to collaborate, communicate, and deliver outcomes. I care most about impact: building momentum toward initiatives that matter.
